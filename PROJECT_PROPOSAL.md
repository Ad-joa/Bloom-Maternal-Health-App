# DESIGN OF A SMART MATERNAL HEALTH ADVISORY MOBILE APPLICATION

## INTRODUCTION AND BACKGROUND
Maternal health has been a critical public health concern globally, with developing countries bearing an excessively high burden of maternal morbidity and mortality. In Ghana, pregnancy-related complications continue to affect the wellbeing of women, particularly those living in rural and underdeveloped communities. Although initiatives such as free maternal healthcare and community-based health services have contributed to improvements, access to timely and accurate maternal health information remains a challenge for many expectant mothers (Adu et al., 2021; Ghana Health Service, 2022).

In many Ghanaian households, pregnant women rely heavily on informal sources of information, including family members, traditional birth attendants, and social media platforms, often delaying professional consultation until conditions become critical. While some of these sources provide emotional and cultural support, the medical accuracy of the information shared is often uncertain and misleading. This issue increases the likelihood of delayed help-seeking, misinterpretation of warning signs, and poor pregnancy outcomes (Opoku-Mensah et al., 2022; UNICEF, 2022).

The rapid growth of internet access and smartphone usage in Ghana presents an opportunity to leverage information technology to support maternal healthcare delivery. According to DataReportal (2023), Ghana continues to record significant growth in mobile internet penetration, creating a viable infrastructure for mobile health interventions. Mobile health advisory applications can provide continuous access to reliable maternal health information beyond clinical visits. By integrating basic intelligent features, such applications can offer personalized guidance that supports informed decision-making during pregnancy (Till et al., 2023).

## PROBLEM STATEMENT 
Despite the availability of maternal healthcare services in Ghana, many pregnant women lack continuous access to reliable and personalized maternal health information outside health facilities. Overcrowded clinics, limited consultation time and equipment, poor road networks to and from healthcare centres, and shortages of healthcare professionals reduce opportunities for expectant mothers to receive adequate guidance throughout pregnancy (Ministry of Health, Ghana, 2021; World Health Organization, 2023).

As a result, minor symptoms that could indicate serious complications are often ignored or treated with herbal remedies, over-the-counter medication, and other traditional methods that could cause serious health complications to both the mother and the baby. Misinformation from informal sources further influences health decisions. There is currently a lack of accessible, user-friendly, and intelligent mobile-based platforms tailored to the maternal health needs of women in Ghana (Kachimanga et al., 2023; Mensah, 2022). This gap necessitates the development of a smart maternal health advisory mobile application that complements existing healthcare services by providing timely, accurate, and context-aware health information.

## AIM AND SPECIFIC OBJECTIVES
The main aim of this project is to design and implement a smart maternal health advisory mobile application that provides pregnant women with accurate, timely, and reliable health information to support informed decision-making during pregnancy.
The specific objectives of the project are to:
- Design a user-friendly mobile-based platform tailored to maternal health education.
- Provide trimester-based maternal health information relevant to various stages of pregnancy.
- Offer symptom-related advisory guidance using basic intelligent logic.
- Promote awareness of pregnancy danger signs and appropriate healthcare-seeking behaviour.
- Evaluate the system based on usability, accessibility, and perceived usefulness.

## PROJECT SCOPE AND LIMITATIONS
The scope of this project covers the design and implementation of a mobile-based maternal health advisory application intended to support pregnant women with health information and general guidance. The system will focus on providing educational content, basic intelligent advisory responses, and awareness of pregnancy-related risks. The application will be designed to complement and not replace professional medical consultation.

Limitations of the project include reliance on predefined health content and simplified intelligent logic rather than advanced machine learning models. The system will also require internet connectivity for certain features, which may limit accessibility for users in areas with poor network coverage (Musiimenta et al., 2022). Additionally, the project timeline may restrict the inclusion of advanced features.

## PROPOSED METHODOLOGY / SYSTEM DESIGN APPROACH
This project will adopt a System Development Life Cycle (SDLC) approach consisting of requirement analysis, system design, implementation, testing, and evaluation. During the requirement analysis phase, relevant maternal health information and user needs will be identified. System design will involve defining the architecture, user interfaces, and data flow of the application.
The mobile application will be developed using React Native, enabling cross-platform deployment on both Android and iOS devices. Python will be used for backend logic and implementation of basic intelligent advisory features. Rule-based decision logic will be applied to provide symptom-related guidance, following established practices in health expert systems (El-Sayed et al., 2023). Testing will focus on functionality and usability across mobile devices, while evaluation will assess system effectiveness and user satisfaction.

## EXPECTED OUTCOMES AND CONTRIBUTIONS
The expected outcome of this project is a functional smart maternal health advisory mobile application that provides reliable and accessible pregnancy-related information to expectant mothers. The system is expected to improve maternal health awareness, encourage early recognition of danger signs, promote frequent antenatal visits, and facilitate timely healthcare-seeking behaviour (Adu et al., 2023).
Academically, the project will demonstrate the application of information technology and basic intelligent systems in addressing public health challenges. Socially, it will contribute to efforts aimed at improving maternal health outcomes in Ghana by supporting informed and responsible pregnancy care, consistent with national and global maternal health priorities (Ministry of Health, Ghana, 2021; World Health Organization, 2023).
